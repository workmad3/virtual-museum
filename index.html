Virtual Museum

Document physical artefacts as a community

Download this project as a .zip file Download this project as a tar.gz file

Why

The initial motivation for Virtual Museum is that I'm looking after the keys to a room of stuff from the very early days of computing. For one reason or another, this collection is never going to make it into a museum setting. Further, the collection is not documented in any way, and the generation of people who know about the items in the collection is growing smaller and smaller. However, this group of people are computer literate and hopefully motivated to document artefacts given a suitable system to do so.

What

Virtual Museum is basically a wiki that is to be used to catalogue and describe related artefacts in a collection. Collection item descriptions can be developed by users, and comments can be used to add related information.

Contributors who don't feel like curating wiki content may prefer to use comments to add information to item descriptions, and later these comments might be gathered and incorporated into the wiki content by people who enjoy curating page content.

Thus we can make adding knowledge into a lightweight operation, add some information or a recollection in a comment to a wiki page that contains an image of something in the collection -- no need to be a wiki page author.

Features

Strikethrough is used to indicate features still to be implemented.

  • A basic authorisation scheme

    • Contributors must have registered themselves as users
    • Commenters may also authenticate with Facebook, Google, Twitter or Linked in
    • there is a class of admin users who can perform a few privileged operations.

  • Wiki pages and their comments support rich content:

    • Text
    • Hyperlinks to other pages
    • URLs to the outside world
    • Images
    • YouTube and Vimeo videos.

  • Wiki pages may be formatted using Markdown, a simple text formatting language.

  • Comments on pages.

  • Page tags.

  • A simple category scheme for pages based on a customisable Linked Data representation of hierarchies of categories.

  • ~~ Page formats according to categories ~~

    • For example, pages categorised as representing a museum artefact contain a unique reference for and the current location of the artefact.
    • Whereas pages categorised as representing a person have neither of these fields.
